This publication draws attention to the way in which human and machine have merged. Technology is giving people who’s bodies have failed them a chance to reconnect with people and communicate again. A topical subject, accessibility is the area of technological advancement where there is little room for debate around whether or not it effects people positively. How can we make human and machine work as one seamlessly for people who will need it in the future. Although the body poses limitations for many, and we can see the effects of this from the increasing development in virtual reality, but we cannot deny that real human communication in the real world is unparalleled. “Locked in” syndrome is possibly the most extreme case in which the person affected feels the lost ability to communicate, and it is these lost people, who when found are the people who are pushing this important area in technology. We must strive to explore how humans and machines can act as one to improve peoples quality of life. A machine can make someone human.
