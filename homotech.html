This publication draws attention to the way in which human and machine have merged. Homo Technican marks the important developments in human communication technology, aiding people who are unable to speak to give them a voice. Locked-in syndrome being the most extreme case of this, we can see that a machine can make someone human, because "communication is what makes us human". Evoking empathy in the reader by dramtically slowing their reading ability with the use of omitting blocks over the text, and the deconstructivist paragrah text was the intention, with the french fold format communicating the appearance on the outside vs. reality on the inside. The inner pages hosting handwritten typography direct onto the printed pages was the most human form of typography to materialise the pull quotes.
